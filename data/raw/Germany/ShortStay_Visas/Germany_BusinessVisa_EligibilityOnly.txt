Source: https://www.germany.info/us-en/service/visa/business-visa-963542
Country: Germany  
Visa Type: Business Visa (Short-Stay Schengen – up to 90 days)  

1. Overview
The German Business Visa (Schengen Visa – Category C) allows individuals to visit Germany for short-term business-related activities such as attending meetings, conferences, negotiations, or signing contracts.  
It permits stays of up to **90 days within a 180-day period** across the Schengen Area.  
This visa does not authorize long-term employment or paid work in Germany.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- The **applicant must be traveling to Germany for legitimate business purposes**, including:  
  • Attending meetings, trade fairs, or conferences.  
  • Negotiating or signing contracts.  
  • Participating in short-term professional training.  
  • Engaging in official or corporate visits.  
- The **intended stay must not exceed 90 days** within a 180-day period.  
- The **applicant must hold a valid passport** and, if required, obtain a Schengen visa prior to travel.  
- **Visa-exempt nationals** (e.g., from the EU, EEA, or visa-free countries) do not need a business visa for short visits.  

3. Sponsorship and Invitation Requirements
- Applicants are often required to provide a **letter of invitation** from the host company or organization in Germany specifying:  
  • Purpose of the visit,  
  • Duration and location of business activities, and  
  • Confirmation of financial responsibility (if applicable).  
- If applicable, the **employer in the home country** should issue a letter confirming the applicant’s position, salary, and purpose of travel.  

4. Financial and Travel Requirements
- The applicant must demonstrate **sufficient financial means** to cover travel, accommodation, and living expenses.  
- Applicants must hold **travel health insurance** valid for the entire Schengen Area with a minimum coverage of **€30,000**.  
- Proof of **round-trip travel reservations** or itinerary showing entry and exit from the Schengen Area is required.  
- The applicant must have **no intention of working or residing in Germany beyond the visa validity**.

5. Application Steps
1. Determine if a Schengen visa is required based on nationality.  
2. Complete the **Schengen Visa Application Form (Category C)** online or on paper.  
3. Schedule an appointment at the **German Embassy or Consulate** in the applicant’s country of residence.  
4. Prepare and submit required documents, including invitation letter, travel itinerary, and financial proof.  
5. Pay the applicable **visa processing fee**.  
6. Attend the visa interview (if required) and provide biometric data.  
7. Await processing; once approved, collect the visa prior to departure.

6. Required Documentation
- Completed **Schengen Visa Application Form (Business Category)**.  
- Valid **passport** (at least three months beyond the planned departure date).  
- **Two biometric photographs** per Schengen standards.  
- **Letter of invitation** from the business partner in Germany.  
- **Proof of business relationship**, such as contracts, conference registration, or correspondence.  
- **Proof of travel arrangements** (flight booking, itinerary).  
- **Proof of accommodation** (hotel booking or host confirmation).  
- **Travel health insurance** covering at least €30,000 in medical emergencies.  
- **Proof of financial means** (bank statements, sponsorship letter, or employer letter).  

7. Validity and Conditions
- The visa allows **short-term stays of up to 90 days** in any 180-day period across the Schengen Area.  
- The visa may be **single-entry, double-entry, or multiple-entry**, depending on the applicant’s purpose and travel history.  
- The holder may engage only in **business-related activities**, not employment or paid work.  
- Extensions are rarely granted except in cases of force majeure or serious personal reasons.

8. Ineligibility Conditions
Applicants may be found ineligible if they:  
- Fail to prove the genuine business purpose of travel.  
- Lack sufficient financial or travel documentation.  
- Have previously overstayed or violated Schengen visa regulations.  
- Are listed in the Schengen Information System (SIS) as inadmissible.  
- Provide incomplete or false information.  

9. Legal References
- **Schengen Visa Code (Regulation (EC) No 810/2009)**.  
- **German Residence Act (Aufenthaltsgesetz)** – Section 6 (Short Stay).  
- **Common Consular Instructions of the Schengen States**.  
- **Federal Foreign Office (Auswärtiges Amt)** – Business Visa Policy Guidelines.  

10. Notes
- Business visa holders are allowed to travel freely within all **Schengen countries** during the visa’s validity.  
- The visa does not permit paid employment or freelance work in Germany.  
- Frequent business travelers may apply for a **multiple-entry visa** valid up to 5 years if eligibility criteria are met.  
- The **processing time** typically ranges from 10 to 15 calendar days.  
- Applicants should apply **at least 15 days before the intended travel date** but no earlier than six months in advance.
