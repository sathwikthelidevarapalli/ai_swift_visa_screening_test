Country: Germany  
Visa Type: Permanent Settlement Permit 

1. Overview
The Permanent Settlement Permit (Niederlassungserlaubnis) allows a foreign national to live and work in Germany indefinitely.  
It is a long-term residence status under the German Residence Act (Aufenthaltsgesetz) granted to individuals who have demonstrated stable residence, financial independence, and integration into German society.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- Applicant must have **legally resided in Germany** for at least **five consecutive years** under a valid residence permit.  
- Applicant must have a **secure and regular income** sufficient to cover living expenses without relying on public funds.  
- Applicant must have **adequate health insurance** coverage.  
- Applicant must provide **proof of retirement or pension provision** ensuring long-term financial stability.  
- Applicant must demonstrate **sufficient German language skills** (minimum **B1 level**) and **basic knowledge of German law, society, and culture**.  
- Applicant must have **adequate housing** that meets German living standards.  
- Applicant must not have a **criminal record** or pose a threat to public security or order.

3. Integration and Language Requirements
- A **German language certificate (B1 CEFR)** is required as proof of integration.  
- Applicants must also show understanding of **German laws, rights, and obligations** through an integration course certificate or equivalent proof.  
- Completion of an **integration course** can reduce the required residence period from **five years to three years** under certain conditions.

4. Financial and Employment Criteria
- Applicant must show continuous **employment or self-employment** in Germany.  
- Proof of **income stability** through payslips, tax returns, or business documents is required.  
- **Public assistance** such as social welfare or unemployment benefits (ALG II) generally disqualifies the applicant.  
- Applicant must have **sufficient pension contributions** (usually 60 months under the German statutory pension scheme) unless exempt.

5. Validity and Benefits
- The Permanent Settlement Permit grants **unlimited residence** in Germany.  
- Holders can **work, study, or establish a business** without restrictions.  
- Provides **freedom of movement** within Germany and short-term travel rights within the Schengen Area.  
- After several years, holders may become eligible for **German citizenship** if other requirements are met.  
- Family members may also apply for **dependent residence permits** based on the holder’s status.

6. Application Process
1. Submit application at the **local Foreigners’ Office (Ausländerbehörde)**.  
2. Provide proof of residence, income, insurance, housing, and integration.  
3. Attend an interview and verification of documents.  
4. Pay the applicable processing fee.  
5. Upon approval, receive the **Permanent Settlement Permit card**.  

7. Required Documentation
- Valid passport or residence permit.  
- Completed application form.  
- Proof of continuous residence (rental contracts, registration certificates).  
- Proof of income and employment (salary slips, tax records, business licenses).  
- Health insurance certificate.  
- Pension or retirement contribution record.  
- German language certificate (B1 level).  
- Integration course completion certificate (if applicable).  
- Proof of adequate housing (rental or ownership documents).  
- No-criminal-record certificate (police clearance).  

8. Ineligibility Conditions
An application may be refused if the applicant:  
- Has not completed the required residence period.  
- Depends on public welfare or fails to prove income stability.  
- Lacks sufficient German language or integration knowledge.  
- Has a criminal record or poses a security threat.  
- Submits incomplete or false documentation.

9. Legal References
- **Residence Act (Aufenthaltsgesetz)** – Section 9 (Permanent Settlement Permit).  
- **Integration Course Ordinance (IntV)** – Language and social integration requirements.  
- **EU Directive 2003/109/EC** – Long-term resident status for third-country nationals.

10. Notes
- Certain categories, such as **highly skilled workers**, **Blue Card holders**, or **graduates of German universities**, may qualify sooner (after 33 months or 21 months with B1 language proficiency).  
- The settlement permit is **not automatically revoked** when changing employers or jobs.  
- Children born in Germany to settlement permit holders may acquire residence rights more easily.  
- Applicants should ensure all official documents are **translated into German** by certified translators.
