Country: Germany  
Visa Type: EU Blue Card (Residence Permit for Highly-Qualified Workers)

1. Overview
The EU Blue Card is a residence permit for highly-qualified non-EU nationals who wish to live and work in Germany.  
It is designed to attract skilled professionals with university degrees or equivalent qualifications to meet Germany’s labor market demands.  
The Blue Card offers favorable conditions, including simplified procedures, easier family reunification, and a pathway to permanent residence.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- The applicant must have a **valid job offer or employment contract** in Germany for a **highly-qualified position** lasting at least six months.  
- The applicant must meet the **minimum salary threshold** applicable for the relevant year:  
  • **€48,300 (2025)** for general occupations.  
  • **€43,759.80 (2025)** for shortage occupations (e.g., engineers, doctors, IT specialists, natural scientists, mathematicians).  
- The applicant must hold a **recognized university degree** or an equivalent higher-education qualification relevant to the position.  
- If applying as an IT professional without a formal degree, the applicant must provide **proof of at least three years of professional experience** within the last seven years in a comparable role.  
- The applicant must demonstrate compliance with **German employment and immigration laws**.

3. Employment and Qualification Requirements
- The employment offer must be **for a qualified position**, typically requiring a degree-level education.  
- The job must correspond to the applicant’s field of study or professional expertise.  
- The salary must meet or exceed the threshold set annually by the Federal Ministry of the Interior.  
- Applicants in shortage occupations benefit from lower salary requirements.  
- Qualifications obtained outside Germany must be either:  
  • Recognized by the **Central Office for Foreign Education (ZAB)**, or  
  • Deemed equivalent to a German university degree under the **Anabin database** system.

4. Financial and Legal Requirements
- The applicant must **earn the minimum required annual gross salary** for eligibility.  
- Employment must comply with German labor standards, including fair working conditions and pay.  
- The employer must verify that the offered role matches the applicant’s qualifications.  
- For positions below the salary threshold, approval from the **Federal Employment Agency (Bundesagentur für Arbeit)** may be required.  

5. Health and Security Requirements
- The applicant must have **valid health insurance** coverage for the entire period of stay.  
- Public or private health insurance must meet the legal standards applicable in Germany.  
- Applicants must **not pose a threat to public order, security, or health**.  
- A clean criminal record and good conduct history are essential.  

6. Validity and Duration
- The EU Blue Card is issued for a period of **up to four years** or the duration of the employment contract plus three months, whichever is shorter.  
- Holders may change employers within the first two years only with approval from the **Foreigners’ Office (Ausländerbehörde)**.  
- After **33 months** of qualified employment and contribution to the pension system, holders may apply for a **permanent settlement permit**.  
- The time requirement may be reduced to **21 months** if the holder demonstrates **B1-level German language proficiency**.  

7. Application Steps
1. Obtain a **qualified job offer or employment contract** in Germany.  
2. Ensure your degree is recognized via the **Anabin** database or through ZAB equivalence verification.  
3. Collect required documentation, including proof of salary, qualifications, and health insurance.  
4. Apply for the **National Visa (D type)** at the **German embassy or consulate** in your home country.  
5. Upon arrival in Germany, register your residence and apply for the **EU Blue Card residence permit** at the **local Foreigners’ Office**.  
6. Receive your residence card and begin employment.  

8. Required Documentation
- Valid passport and recent biometric photographs.  
- Completed and signed **EU Blue Card visa application form**.  
- Employment contract or official job offer specifying salary and duration.  
- Proof of recognized qualifications (university degree or ZAB recognition).  
- Proof of health insurance coverage.  
- Proof of salary meeting the legal threshold.  
- Curriculum vitae (CV) and supporting professional documents.  
- Proof of accommodation in Germany.  
- Visa fee payment receipt.  

9. Ineligibility Conditions
Applicants may be ineligible if they:  
- Do not meet the minimum salary requirement.  
- Fail to prove qualification recognition or professional experience.  
- Intend to work in an unqualified or unrelated position.  
- Provide false or incomplete documentation.  
- Have previously violated immigration or employment laws in Germany.  

10. Legal References
- **German Residence Act (Aufenthaltsgesetz)** – Section 18g: EU Blue Card.  
- **Directive 2009/50/EC** of the European Council – Conditions for entry and residence of third-country nationals for highly qualified employment.  
- **Make-it-in-Germany Portal** – Federal Government official guidance for skilled workers.  

11. Notes
- The EU Blue Card provides a clear path to **permanent residency** in Germany.  
- Holders may bring family members under simplified reunification rules.  
- Time spent under the Blue Card in other EU countries may count toward permanent residence in the EU.  
- Blue Card holders enjoy labor mobility within the EU after **18 months of residence** in the first member state.  
- Renewal applications should be submitted at least **eight weeks before expiration** to avoid gaps in legal residence status.
