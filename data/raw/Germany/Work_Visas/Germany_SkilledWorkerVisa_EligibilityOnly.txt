Country: Germany  
Visa Type: Skilled Worker Visa (National Visa / Residence Permit for Qualified Professionals)

1. Overview
The Skilled Worker Visa (National Visa / Residence Permit) allows qualified non-EU nationals to live and work in Germany in a profession that matches their recognized qualifications.  
This visa aims to address skilled labor shortages in key sectors such as engineering, healthcare, manufacturing, and IT.  
It also provides a pathway to long-term residence and, in some cases, permanent settlement for professionals contributing to the German economy.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- The applicant must hold a **recognized foreign vocational qualification** or **university degree** equivalent to a German qualification.  
- The applicant must have a **valid job offer or employment contract** in Germany that corresponds to their professional qualifications.  
- If aged **45 years or older** and entering Germany for the first time, the applicant must:  
  • Earn a **minimum gross annual salary of €53,130 (as of 2025)**, or  
  • Provide **proof of adequate pension or retirement provisions**.  
- The applicant must meet all **legal and professional requirements** applicable to the intended occupation (such as licenses or registrations for regulated professions).  

3. Qualification and Recognition Requirements
- The applicant’s educational or professional qualification must be **formally recognized in Germany**.  
- Recognition can be verified through the **Anabin database** (for academic degrees) or through the **Recognition Portal (Anerkennung in Deutschland)** for vocational qualifications.  
- If the qualification is only partially equivalent, the applicant may be eligible for **adaptation or compensation training** to obtain full recognition.  
- Applicants with German degrees are automatically recognized.  

4. Employment and Salary Requirements
- The employment offer must be **directly related to the applicant’s qualification**.  
- The job must meet German employment standards and comply with labor laws.  
- The **Federal Employment Agency (Bundesagentur für Arbeit)** may need to approve the position to ensure:  
  • Comparable wages and working conditions to German employees.  
  • No adverse effects on the domestic labor market.  
- Applicants aged 45+ must meet the **minimum salary threshold** or demonstrate adequate pension coverage.

5. Financial and Health Requirements
- The applicant must be financially self-sufficient and able to support themselves and any dependents without relying on public assistance.  
- Valid **health insurance coverage** (public or private) must be maintained throughout the stay in Germany.  
- Proof of accommodation in Germany (rental agreement or employer-provided housing) is required at the time of application.

6. Validity and Duration
- The Skilled Worker Visa is initially issued as a **National Visa (D)** valid for entry and short-term residence (usually up to 90 days).  
- Upon arrival, the applicant must apply for a **residence permit for employment purposes** under **Section 18a or 18b of the Residence Act**.  
- The residence permit is generally valid for the duration of the employment contract, up to **four years**, and renewable.  
- After several years of legal residence and employment, holders may qualify for a **permanent settlement permit (Niederlassungserlaubnis)**.

7. Application Steps
1. Obtain formal **recognition of qualification** (if required).  
2. Secure a **job offer or employment contract** from a German employer.  
3. Gather all supporting documents (proof of qualifications, recognition, salary, insurance, accommodation).  
4. Complete and sign the **National Visa Application Form**.  
5. Submit the visa application to the **German embassy or consulate** in your home country.  
6. Once approved, travel to Germany and register your residence at the **Einwohnermeldeamt**.  
7. Apply for a **residence permit** at the local **Foreigners’ Office (Ausländerbehörde)**.

8. Required Documentation
- Valid passport.  
- Completed and signed visa application form.  
- Recent biometric photographs.  
- Recognized qualification or degree certificate.  
- Proof of qualification recognition (Anabin/ZAB certificate).  
- Employment contract or offer specifying job role and salary.  
- Proof of adequate health insurance.  
- Proof of accommodation in Germany.  
- Curriculum vitae (CV) detailing education and work experience.  
- Proof of financial means (bank statements, salary slips, or contract).  
- Visa fee payment receipt.  

9. Ineligibility Conditions
Applicants may be found ineligible if they:  
- Fail to prove qualification recognition or equivalence.  
- Do not have a valid job offer aligned with their qualification.  
- Cannot demonstrate sufficient financial means or health insurance.  
- Provide false or incomplete documentation.  
- Pose a risk to public security, health, or order under German immigration law.  

10. Legal References
- **German Residence Act (Aufenthaltsgesetz)** – Section 18a (Vocational Training) and 18b (Academic Professionals).  
- **Employment Regulation (BeschV)** – Rules for foreign worker access to the labor market.  
- **Recognition Act (Anerkennungsgesetz)** – Recognition of foreign qualifications.  
- **Make-it-in-Germany** – Official federal government portal for skilled migration.  

11. Notes
- Skilled workers may bring family members under the **Family Reunification** provisions.  
- After two years of residence under Section 18b, applicants may change employers or professions without new approval.  
- Time spent working as a skilled professional counts toward permanent settlement eligibility.  
- Language proficiency at **A2–B1 level** may be required for residence permit renewal or integration purposes.  
- Applicants are encouraged to begin the recognition process before applying for a visa to reduce processing delays.
