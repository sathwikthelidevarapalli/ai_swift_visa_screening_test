Country: Germany  
Visa Type: Job-Seeker Visa (National Visa – for seeking employment in Germany)

1. Overview
The Job-Seeker Visa (National Visa – Type D) allows qualified non-EU nationals to travel to Germany for the purpose of seeking employment.  
This visa is intended for skilled professionals and university graduates who wish to explore job opportunities in Germany before obtaining a work or residence permit.  
It does not authorize employment but provides a legal stay for up to six months while searching for a job matching the applicant’s qualifications.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- The applicant must hold a **university degree** recognized in Germany or equivalent to a German higher education qualification.  
- The applicant must demonstrate **sufficient financial resources** to support themselves during the stay without recourse to public funds.  
- The applicant must **intend to stay solely for job searching** purposes and must leave Germany if no employment is found within the visa’s validity period.  
- The applicant must have **comprehensive health insurance coverage** valid throughout the stay in Germany.  
- The applicant must meet general entry and security requirements under the **German Residence Act (AufenthG)**.  

3. Educational and Qualification Requirements
- The applicant’s foreign degree must be **recognized or equivalent** to a German higher education qualification.  
- Recognition can be verified through the **Anabin database** or the **Zentralstelle für ausländisches Bildungswesen (ZAB)**.  
- Applicants with degrees from German universities are automatically considered eligible.  
- If a degree is not listed in Anabin, official recognition documentation from ZAB must be presented.  

4. Financial Requirements
- Applicants must prove access to **sufficient financial means** to cover living costs during their job-seeking stay.  
- The minimum amount required is approximately **€1,091 per month** (as of 2025), typically shown through a **blocked bank account** or sponsorship letter.  
- Other acceptable proofs include:  
  • Bank statements showing sufficient funds.  
  • Official declaration of commitment (Verpflichtungserklärung) from a sponsor residing in Germany.  

5. Validity and Duration
- The Job-Seeker Visa is granted for a **maximum of six months**.  
- During this time, **employment or self-employment is not permitted**.  
- If the applicant finds suitable employment, they must apply for a **residence permit for employment purposes** before the visa expires.  
- If no employment is secured within six months, the applicant must leave Germany but may reapply later if eligibility continues to be met.  

6. Application Steps
1. Verify that your **degree is recognized** in Germany via the Anabin or ZAB system.  
2. Gather all required documents (proof of funds, qualifications, health insurance, accommodation).  
3. Complete the **National Visa Application Form**.  
4. Schedule and attend an appointment at the **German embassy or consulate** in your country of residence.  
5. Submit your visa application and biometric data.  
6. Wait for processing (usually 4–8 weeks).  
7. Upon visa approval, travel to Germany and begin your job search.  

7. Required Documentation
- Valid passport.  
- Completed and signed visa application form.  
- Recent biometric passport photographs.  
- Proof of academic qualification (degree certificate and recognition proof).  
- Proof of sufficient financial means (blocked account, bank statements, or sponsorship letter).  
- Proof of health insurance valid in Germany.  
- Proof of accommodation (rental contract or letter of stay).  
- Curriculum vitae (CV) and motivation letter explaining job-seeking plans.  
- Visa fee payment receipt.  

8. Ineligibility Conditions
Applicants may be found ineligible if they:  
- Fail to provide recognized proof of academic qualifications.  
- Do not demonstrate sufficient financial means or health insurance coverage.  
- Intend to engage in employment before obtaining a work permit.  
- Have previously overstayed or violated immigration laws in Germany or the EU.  
- Submit incomplete, false, or inconsistent documentation.  

9. Legal References
- **German Residence Act (Aufenthaltsgesetz)** – Section 20: Residence for the Purpose of Seeking Employment.  
- **Federal Employment Agency (Bundesagentur für Arbeit)** – Guidelines for labor market access.  
- **German Federal Foreign Office (Auswärtiges Amt)** – Official FAQ on employment and visa regulations.  

10. Notes
- The Job-Seeker Visa does not permit part-time or freelance work during the search period.  
- After securing employment, the applicant must apply for a **work visa** or **EU Blue Card** at the local **Foreigners’ Office (Ausländerbehörde)**.  
- Reapplication is possible after leaving Germany, provided eligibility continues to be met.  
- Applicants are encouraged to research job opportunities and prepare applications before arrival.  
- Proof of professional experience and language proficiency (German or English) may strengthen the application.
