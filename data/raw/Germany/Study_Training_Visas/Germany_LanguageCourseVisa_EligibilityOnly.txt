Country: Germany  
Visa Type: Language Course Visa (National Visa – for participation in an intensive German language course)

1. Overview
The Language Course Visa (National Visa – Category D) allows foreign nationals to reside in Germany for the purpose of participating in an **intensive German language course**.  
This visa is intended solely for language acquisition and not for employment or academic degree programs.  
The course must take place in Germany and meet the required standards of intensity and duration as specified by the German authorities.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- The **applicant must be enrolled** in an **intensive German language course** consisting of at least **18 hours of instruction per week**.  
- The language course must be **full-time** and aimed at improving general language skills or preparing for university studies or professional integration.  
- The **course duration must exceed 3 months**; for courses under 90 days, a Schengen visa is generally sufficient.  
- The visa does not allow for conversion to a student visa after arrival unless explicitly stated by the immigration authority.

3. Financial Requirements
- The **applicant must demonstrate sufficient financial means** to support themselves during their stay in Germany, without the need for public assistance.  
- The minimum proof of funds required is approximately **€1,091 per month** (as of 2025).  
- Proof can be provided through:  
  • A **blocked account (Sperrkonto)** in a German bank showing adequate funds for the entire stay, or  
  • A **formal obligation letter (Verpflichtungserklärung)** from a financial sponsor residing in Germany.

4. Health Insurance and Accommodation
- The **applicant must have valid health insurance** covering the entire duration of the stay in Germany.  
- Travel health insurance is acceptable for the initial entry but must be replaced by long-term German health insurance upon arrival.  
- Proof of **accommodation** (rental agreement, host invitation, or dormitory confirmation) is required at the time of application.

5. Application Steps
1. Complete the **National Visa Application Form (Category D)**.  
2. Schedule a **visa appointment** at the German Embassy or Consulate in the applicant’s home country.  
3. Prepare and submit all required documentation, including proof of enrollment, financial means, and insurance.  
4. Attend the visa interview and pay the visa fee.  
5. Await processing; after approval, collect the visa for entry into Germany.  
6. Upon arrival, register the address at the **Einwohnermeldeamt** and apply for a residence permit at the **Ausländerbehörde** if staying longer than 90 days.

6. Required Documentation
- Valid **passport** and **completed visa application form**.  
- **Letter of admission or enrollment** from the German language school specifying duration and weekly hours.  
- **Proof of financial means** (blocked account or sponsorship letter).  
- **Proof of accommodation** for the duration of the course.  
- **Health insurance certificate** valid for the entire stay.  
- **Motivation letter** explaining purpose of study and future plans.  
- **Proof of previous education or language proficiency**, if applicable.  
- **Visa fee payment receipt** and biometric photographs.

7. Validity and Conditions
- The Language Course Visa is typically issued for **3 to 12 months**, depending on the course duration.  
- It **cannot be extended** or converted into another residence title in Germany unless the course directly transitions to a recognized educational program.  
- Employment is **not permitted** during the stay under this visa.  
- Applicants must attend the language course regularly and maintain residence registration.

8. Ineligibility Conditions
Applicants may be found ineligible if they:  
- Fail to provide valid proof of enrollment in an intensive course.  
- Lack sufficient financial means or proof of health insurance.  
- Have previously overstayed or violated German visa conditions.  
- Intend to use the visa for purposes other than language study.  
- Submit incomplete or falsified documentation.

9. Legal References
- **Residence Act (Aufenthaltsgesetz)** – Section 16f: Study Preparation and Language Acquisition.  
- **Ordinance on the Employment of Foreigners (BeschV)** – Employment Restrictions for Language Course Holders.  
- **German Federal Foreign Office (Auswärtiges Amt)** – Visa Information Portal for Language Course Visas.  

10. Notes
- This visa is ideal for applicants preparing to study or work in Germany by improving their language skills.  
- Attendance and completion certificates from the course are often required for future study visa applications.  
- Holders of this visa are encouraged to complete an integration course if available.  
- Applications should be submitted **at least 6 to 8 weeks before the intended travel date**.
