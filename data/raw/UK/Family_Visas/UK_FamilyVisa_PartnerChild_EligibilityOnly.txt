Source: https://www.gov.uk/uk-family-visa/partner-spouse
Country: United Kingdom
Visa Type: Partner or Spouse Visa (Family Visa)

1. Overview
The Partner or Spouse Visa allows the spouse, civil partner, fiancé(e), or long-term partner of a person settled in the United Kingdom to join them and live together permanently.  
This visa forms part of the UK Family Visa route under Appendix FM of the Immigration Rules.  
It ensures family unity for British citizens, settled residents, and persons with humanitarian protection.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- Both the applicant and the partner must be **aged 18 years or older**.  
- The **UK-based partner (sponsor)** must be one of the following:  
  • A **British or Irish citizen**.  
  • **Settled in the UK** (holding Indefinite Leave to Remain, Settled Status, or permanent residence).  
  • From the **EU, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, or Liechtenstein** with **pre-settled status**, provided the relationship began before **1 January 2021**.  
  • A **Turkish Businessperson or Worker visa holder**.  
  • A **refugee or individual granted humanitarian protection** in the UK.  
- The applicant and partner must intend to **live together permanently** in the UK.  
- The relationship must be one of the following:  
  • A **marriage or civil partnership** legally recognised in the UK.  
  • A **cohabiting relationship** of at least **2 years** comparable to marriage.  
  • An **engagement or proposed civil partnership**, where marriage or registration will take place within **6 months** of arrival.  

3. Relationship and Genuine Intent Requirements
- The relationship must be **genuine and subsisting**.  
- Evidence such as photographs, communication history, joint financial commitments, or cohabitation records may be required.  
- The couple must demonstrate a **shared intention** to reside together in the UK on a permanent basis.  
- Previous marriages or civil partnerships must have been legally dissolved before application.  

4. Financial Requirements
- The sponsor must meet the **minimum income threshold** of **£18,600 per year**.  
- Additional financial requirements apply if dependent children are included.  
- Income can come from employment, self-employment, pensions, or savings (minimum £62,500 savings if income threshold not met).  
- Acceptable evidence includes payslips, tax returns, bank statements, and employment letters.  
- Applicants may rely on combined income and savings if applicable.  

5. English Language Requirement
- The applicant must prove **English language ability** at **A1 level or higher** of the Common European Framework (CEFR) through:  
  • An approved English language test, OR  
  • A degree taught in English, OR  
  • Citizenship from a majority English-speaking country.  
- Certain exemptions apply for age, disability, or exceptional circumstances.  

6. Accommodation Requirements
- The couple must have **adequate accommodation** available in the UK that meets public health and overcrowding standards.  
- Proof such as tenancy agreements, mortgage statements, or property inspection reports may be required.  

7. Validity and Duration
- A Partner or Spouse Visa issued from outside the UK is valid for **33 months**.  
- If applied for within the UK (switching category), it is valid for **30 months**.  
- The visa can be extended for another **30 months**, after which applicants may qualify for **Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)**.  
- Fiancé(e) or proposed civil partner visas are valid for **6 months**, during which marriage or registration must take place.  

8. Application Steps
1. Confirm eligibility and gather relationship, financial, and accommodation evidence.  
2. Complete the **online application** on the UK Government website.  
3. Pay the **visa fee** and **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)**.  
4. Schedule and attend a **biometric appointment** at a Visa Application Centre (VAC).  
5. Submit all supporting documentation electronically or in person.  
6. Await decision from **UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI)**.  

9. Required Documentation
- Valid passport or travel document.  
- Proof of genuine relationship (marriage certificate, photos, communication logs).  
- Financial documents (payslips, bank statements, tax records).  
- Accommodation proof (rental agreement or property ownership).  
- Proof of English language proficiency (test certificate or degree).  
- Sponsor’s proof of status in the UK (passport, BRP, or residence permit).  
- Payment receipts for visa fees and IHS.  
- Tuberculosis (TB) test certificate if applicable.  

10. Ineligibility Conditions
Applicants may be refused if they:  
- Fail to prove a genuine and subsisting relationship.  
- Do not meet the financial or language requirements.  
- Provide false, incomplete, or misleading documentation.  
- Have overstayed or violated UK immigration laws previously.  
- Are inadmissible on medical, security, or public order grounds.  

11. Legal References
- **Immigration Rules Appendix FM** – Family Members.  
- **Appendix FM-SE** – Specified Evidence Requirements.  
- **Home Office Partner and Spouse Policy Guidance** – Relationship verification and maintenance criteria.  
- **Immigration Act 1971** and **Nationality, Immigration and Asylum Act 2002**.  

12. Notes
- Applicants may switch to the Partner route within the UK if holding a valid visa in an eligible category.  
- After five years of continuous residence under this route, applicants may apply for **Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)**.  
- Spouses of British citizens may later qualify for **British citizenship** through naturalization.  
- Refused applicants can appeal to the **First-tier Tribunal (Immigration and Asylum Chamber)** if family life rights under Article 8 ECHR are engaged.
