Source: https://www.gov.uk/parent-of-a-child-at-school-visa
Country: United Kingdom
Visa Type: Parent of a Child Student Visa (Family Visa)

1. Overview
The Parent of a Child Student Visa allows one parent to accompany their child who is studying in the United Kingdom under the Child Student Visa category.  
It enables parents to provide care and supervision for their child enrolled at an independent fee-paying day school in the UK.  
This visa is temporary and does not provide a direct route to settlement.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- The applicant must be **at least 18 years old**.  
- The applicant must be the **parent of a child aged between 4 and 11 years old** who:  
  • Holds a valid **Child Student Visa**, or  
  • Is **applying for one** at the same time.  
- The applicant must be the **only parent** accompanying the child in the UK.  
  • The other parent must **live outside the UK** and cannot apply to join.  
- The applicant must have **sufficient financial resources** to support themselves and their child without recourse to public funds.  
- The applicant must **maintain their main home outside the UK**.  
- The applicant must intend to **leave the UK when the visa expires** or when the child ceases to qualify under the scheme.

3. Financial and Accommodation Requirements
- The applicant must demonstrate **adequate financial means** to cover living costs for both themselves and the child without public assistance.  
- Acceptable financial evidence includes bank statements, proof of regular income, or savings documentation.  
- The applicant must provide evidence of **suitable accommodation** for themselves and the child during their stay in the UK.

4. Duration and Conditions of Stay
- The visa is granted for a **maximum of 12 months** at a time or until the child’s visa expires, whichever is shorter.  
- The visa can be **renewed** annually while the child continues to study at an eligible institution and remains within the age range.  
- The parent **cannot work, study, or access public funds** in the UK.  
- The parent **cannot bring other family members** (such as a spouse or additional children) to the UK under this route.  
- The parent must **live with the child** and actively care for them during their stay.

5. Application Steps
1. Complete the **online application** form on the UK Government website.  
2. Pay the **visa application fee** and the **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)**.  
3. Book and attend a **biometric appointment** at a Visa Application Centre (VAC).  
4. Submit supporting documents, including proof of relationship, accommodation, and financial capability.  
5. Wait for a decision from **UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI)**.

6. Required Documentation
- Valid passport or travel document.  
- Proof of parental relationship (birth certificate of the child).  
- Proof of the child’s current or pending **Child Student Visa**.  
- Evidence of financial means (bank statements, payslips, or savings).  
- Proof of accommodation in the UK.  
- Evidence of the applicant’s **main home outside the UK** (e.g., property documents, tenancy agreement).  
- Tuberculosis (TB) test certificate if applicable.  
- Visa fee and IHS payment receipts.

7. Ineligibility Conditions
Applicants may be refused if they:  
- Fail to prove genuine parental relationship or custody of the child.  
- Lack sufficient funds or accommodation.  
- Are not the sole accompanying parent.  
- Have violated previous UK immigration laws or overstayed a visa.  
- Fail to demonstrate intent to leave the UK after the visa expires.

8. Validity and Renewal
- The visa is normally valid for **up to 12 months**, renewable while the child remains eligible.  
- Parents must leave the UK or switch to another eligible category (if permitted) once the child reaches **12 years of age** or ceases study at an approved school.  
- The visa does **not lead to Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)** or permanent residence.

9. Legal References
- **Immigration Rules Appendix Parent of a Child Student.**  
- **Home Office Guidance:** Parent of a Child Student route.  
- **Immigration Act 1971** and **Immigration Rules Part 8 – Family Members.**

10. Notes
- This visa category is designed solely to enable a parent to care for a young child studying in the UK.  
- The visa holder must maintain a **primary residence outside the UK**.  
- Parents are not allowed to switch to work or study routes from within the UK under this category.  
- Renewal requires ongoing compliance with financial and residence conditions.
