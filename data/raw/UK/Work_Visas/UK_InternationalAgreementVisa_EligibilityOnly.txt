Source: https://www.gov.uk/international-agreement-worker-visa
Country: United Kingdom
Visa Type: International Agreement Visa (Temporary Work)

1. Overview  
The **International Agreement Worker Visa** allows individuals to come to the United Kingdom temporarily to perform work covered by international agreements.  
This includes roles such as employees of overseas governments, international organizations, or private servants in diplomatic households.  
It is a **temporary work route**, generally suitable for those providing services under international law or treaty obligations.  

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements  
- The applicant must have a valid **Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)** from a **UK-licensed sponsor** authorized to employ workers under the International Agreement route.  
- The applicant must be **at least 18 years old** at the time of application.  
- The applicant must be coming to work under an **eligible international agreement**, such as:  
  • Employment by a **foreign government**.  
  • Employment with an **international organization**.  
  • Work as a **private servant in a diplomatic household**.  
  • Provision of services covered by a **trade or international agreement**.  
- The applicant must have sufficient **personal savings** to support themselves during their stay (at least **£1,270** held for **28 consecutive days**) unless maintenance is certified by the sponsor.  
- The applicant must **apply from outside the UK**, as switching to this visa from within the UK is **not permitted**.  

3. Financial and Maintenance Requirements  
- The applicant must show proof of financial means to support themselves for the first month in the UK unless their employer certifies maintenance on the Certificate of Sponsorship.  
- Dependents (spouse/partner and children) may accompany the applicant if they meet the financial and eligibility criteria.  
- The applicant must not rely on public funds during their stay.  

4. Duration and Conditions of Stay  
- The visa is granted for the **duration specified on the CoS**, up to a maximum of **2 years**, or the length of the contract (whichever is shorter).  
- The applicant may enter the UK up to **14 days before** the employment start date listed on the CoS.  
- The visa may be extended or renewed if the employment contract and CoS remain valid.  
- The visa holder may work only for the sponsor named on the CoS and must comply with UK immigration rules.  

5. Work Permissions  
- Holders of this visa may:  
  • Work for the sponsoring employer in the approved role.  
  • Undertake voluntary work or study while in the UK (subject to restrictions).  
  • Travel in and out of the UK during the visa validity period.  
- Holders may not:  
  • Take up secondary employment.  
  • Access public funds or state benefits.  
  • Apply for settlement (Indefinite Leave to Remain) directly under this route.  

6. Application Steps  
1. Obtain a **Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)** from a UK-licensed sponsor.  
2. Prepare the required documentation (proof of identity, finances, and job details).  
3. Apply online through the **UK Government visa portal** before traveling to the UK.  
4. Pay the visa application fee and **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)**.  
5. Attend a **biometric appointment** at a visa application centre.  
6. Wait for a decision from **UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI)**.  

7. Required Documentation  
- Valid passport or travel document.  
- Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) reference number.  
- Proof of financial savings or certified maintenance.  
- Employment contract or letter from the sponsor.  
- Tuberculosis (TB) test results (if required).  
- Biometric information (fingerprints and photograph).  

8. Ineligibility Conditions  
An application may be refused if the applicant:  
- Fails to obtain a valid Certificate of Sponsorship.  
- Does not meet the age or financial requirements.  
- Attempts to switch to this visa from within the UK.  
- Provides false or incomplete documentation.  
- Is in breach of UK immigration or criminal laws.  

9. Legal References  
- **Immigration Rules: Appendix Temporary Work – International Agreement Worker**.  
- **Immigration Rules: Appendix Finance**.  
- **UK Home Office Sponsor Guidance (2024 Edition)**.  
- **Immigration Act 1971** and subsequent amendments.  

10. Notes  
- Applicants must apply and receive approval before entering the UK.  
- Dependents can apply to join or accompany the main visa holder if eligible.  
- This visa does not directly lead to permanent residence; however, switching to other eligible work routes may be possible after returning abroad.  
- Sponsors are required to comply with UKVI reporting obligations and maintain accurate employment records.
