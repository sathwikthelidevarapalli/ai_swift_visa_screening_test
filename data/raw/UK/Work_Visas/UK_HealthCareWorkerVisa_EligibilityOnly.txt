Source: https://www.gov.uk/health-care-worker-visa
Country: United Kingdom
Visa Type: Health and Care Worker Visa

1. Overview  
The **Health and Care Worker Visa** allows qualified doctors, nurses, health professionals, and adult social care workers to come to or remain in the United Kingdom to work in eligible health and care sector roles.  
This visa operates under the **Skilled Worker route** and offers a pathway to permanent residence (Indefinite Leave to Remain) after a qualifying period of lawful stay.  

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements  
- The applicant must apply under the **Skilled Worker route** for an occupation in an **eligible health or social care role**.  
- The job offer must be from an **employer approved by the Home Office** (a licensed sponsor).  
- The applicant must hold a valid **Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)** from the sponsoring employer specifying job details and duration.  
- The role must be listed under the **eligible Standard Occupational Classification (SOC) codes** approved for this visa.  
- The applicant must meet the **English language proficiency requirement** as defined under the Skilled Worker rules.  
- The applicant must meet or exceed the **minimum salary threshold** or the **“going rate”** for their specific occupation code.  

3. Eligible Professions and Employers  
- Typical eligible professions include:  
  • Doctors and general practitioners.  
  • Registered nurses and midwives.  
  • Health professionals such as physiotherapists, radiographers, and occupational therapists.  
  • Adult social care workers, senior care assistants, and social workers.  
- The employer must be an organization such as:  
  • NHS (National Health Service).  
  • An organization providing medical services to the NHS.  
  • An organization providing adult social care.  

4. Financial and Maintenance Requirements  
- Applicants must demonstrate the ability to **support themselves financially** upon arrival unless their sponsor certifies maintenance on their behalf.  
- Proof of available funds (bank statements or employer confirmation) may be required.  
- Dependents (spouse/partner and children) may accompany the applicant if they meet the financial and eligibility conditions.  

5. Health and Care Worker Visa Benefits  
- Reduced visa fees compared to the general Skilled Worker visa.  
- Exemption from paying the **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)**.  
- Eligibility to work in the UK for up to **5 years**, with the option to extend or apply for **Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)**.  
- Permission to bring dependents (family members).  
- Ability to switch employers or roles (subject to visa conditions and sponsor approval).  

6. Application Steps  
1. Obtain a **Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)** from a licensed employer.  
2. Prepare and submit the **visa application online** through the UK government portal.  
3. Pay the **visa application fee** (lower than the Skilled Worker Visa fee).  
4. Provide supporting documents including identity proof, CoS reference, and proof of English proficiency.  
5. Attend a **biometric appointment** at a visa application centre.  
6. Wait for a decision from UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI).  

7. Required Documentation  
- Valid passport or travel document.  
- Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) reference number.  
- Proof of English language proficiency (IELTS or equivalent).  
- Evidence of salary meeting the required threshold.  
- Proof of financial support (unless employer certifies maintenance).  
- Tuberculosis (TB) test results (if required).  
- Criminal record certificate (for specific occupations or regions).  

8. Ineligibility Conditions  
An application may be refused if the applicant:  
- Does not hold an eligible job offer or approved CoS.  
- Fails to meet the salary or English language requirement.  
- Has previously breached UK immigration laws.  
- Provides incomplete or fraudulent documentation.  
- Does not meet suitability or admissibility requirements under the Immigration Rules.  

9. Legal References  
- **Immigration Rules: Appendix Skilled Worker**.  
- **Immigration Rules: Appendix Skilled Occupations**.  
- **UK Home Office Sponsor Guidance for Health and Care Workers (2024 Edition)**.  
- **Immigration Act 1971** and associated statutory instruments.  

10. Notes  
- The visa holder can switch employers in the same occupation code or apply for a new visa if taking a different eligible job.  
- Applicants are encouraged to maintain valid health insurance until registered with the NHS.  
- After 5 years of lawful residence under this route, applicants may qualify for **Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)**.  
- Dependents have work and study rights while in the UK.
