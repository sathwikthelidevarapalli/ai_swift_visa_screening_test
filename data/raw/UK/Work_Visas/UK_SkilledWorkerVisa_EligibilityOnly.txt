Source: https://www.gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa
Country: United Kingdom
Visa Type: Skilled Worker Visa

1. Overview  
The **Skilled Worker Visa** allows foreign nationals to live and work in the United Kingdom for an approved employer in an eligible occupation.  
This visa replaced the former Tier 2 (General) work visa and serves as one of the main routes for skilled migration to the UK.  
It enables qualified workers to take up long-term employment and may lead to **Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)** after five years of continuous lawful residence.  

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements  
- The applicant must have a **confirmed job offer** from a **Home Office–licensed sponsor** (a UK employer approved to hire foreign workers).  
- The offered position must fall under an **eligible occupation code** and meet the **required skill level** (RQF Level 3 or above, equivalent to A-level qualifications).  
- The applicant must meet the **minimum salary requirement**, which is the **higher of**:  
  • The **general salary threshold** (£38,700 per year as of 2025), or  
  • The **“going rate”** for the specific job as defined by the UK Home Office occupation codes.  
- A valid **Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)** must be issued by the sponsoring employer detailing the role and salary.  
- The applicant must demonstrate **English language proficiency** (ability to speak, read, write, and understand English) at **CEFR Level B1** or above.  
- The applicant must have sufficient **personal funds** to support themselves unless the employer certifies maintenance on the CoS.  

3. Financial and Maintenance Requirements  
- Applicants must show evidence of **personal savings** of at least **£1,270**, held for **28 consecutive days** before applying, unless their sponsor provides maintenance.  
- Additional funds may be required for dependents:  
  • £285 for a spouse or partner.  
  • £315 for the first child.  
  • £200 for each additional child.  
- Dependents can apply to join or accompany the main visa holder and are permitted to work or study in the UK.  

4. Duration and Conditions of Stay  
- The Skilled Worker Visa is typically granted for up to **5 years** or the duration of the employment contract (whichever is shorter).  
- Visa holders may **extend or renew** their visa as long as they continue to meet sponsorship and employment conditions.  
- After **5 years of continuous residence**, holders may be eligible to apply for **Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)**.  
- Applicants must not rely on public funds and must comply with all UK immigration laws during their stay.  

5. Work and Study Rights  
- Visa holders may:  
  • Work in the sponsored occupation for the approved employer.  
  • Undertake voluntary or supplementary work (up to 20 hours per week in eligible roles).  
  • Study or attend training courses.  
  • Travel freely in and out of the UK.  
- They may not:  
  • Change employer without a new sponsorship and visa update.  
  • Take employment outside the permitted occupation code.  
  • Access public benefits.  

6. Application Steps  
1. Obtain a **Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)** from a licensed UK employer.  
2. Prepare required documentation including proof of English language ability and financial means.  
3. Apply online via the **UK Government Skilled Worker Visa portal**.  
4. Pay the visa application fee and **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)**.  
5. Book and attend a **biometric appointment** at a visa application centre.  
6. Wait for a decision from **UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI)** — typically within 3–8 weeks.  

7. Required Documentation  
- Valid passport or travel document.  
- Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) reference number.  
- Proof of English language proficiency (IELTS, degree taught in English, or UK NARIC certificate).  
- Proof of financial maintenance (bank statements or sponsor certification).  
- Employment contract or job offer letter.  
- Tuberculosis (TB) test results (if applicable).  
- Criminal record certificate (for specific occupations).  

8. Ineligibility Conditions  
Applications may be refused if the applicant:  
- Fails to secure a valid CoS from a licensed sponsor.  
- Does not meet the salary or skill level requirements.  
- Provides false or incomplete documentation.  
- Lacks proof of English language ability.  
- Has breached UK immigration laws or overstayed a previous visa.  

9. Legal References

10. Notes  
- The visa route can lead to **permanent settlement** (ILR) after 5 years of continuous employment.  
- Sponsors must comply with reporting duties and ensure the role continues to meet eligibility criteria.  
- Holders may bring dependents who have access to work and study rights.  
- Salary and occupation code thresholds are reviewed annually by the UK Home Office.  
- Switching from other visa routes (e.g., Student Visa) to Skilled Worker is allowed if eligibility is met.
