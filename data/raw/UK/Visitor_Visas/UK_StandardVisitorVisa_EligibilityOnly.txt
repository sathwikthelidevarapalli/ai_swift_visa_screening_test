Source: https://www.gov.uk/standard-visitor 
Country: United Kingdom
Visa Type: Standard Visitor Visa (Visitor Visa)

1. Overview  
The **UK Standard Visitor Visa** allows individuals to travel to the United Kingdom for short-term visits such as tourism, business, study (up to 6 months), or visiting family and friends.  
This visa does not permit employment or long-term stay in the UK and is typically issued for a duration of **up to 6 months** per visit.  

It is the most common visa category for short-term entry and can also be used for medical treatment or other approved temporary purposes.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements  
- The applicant must hold a **valid passport or travel document** that covers the entire duration of stay in the UK.  
- The applicant must **intend to leave the UK** at the end of their visit.  
- The applicant must be able to **financially support themselves and any dependents** during the stay, or have adequate funding from a verified sponsor.  
- The applicant must **not live in the UK through frequent or successive visits**.  
- The applicant must **not take up paid or unpaid employment**, or undertake long-term study or marriage registration, unless permitted under specific exceptions.  
- The applicant must **apply from outside the UK** and **meet all immigration health and security requirements**.  

3. Permitted Activities  
Standard visitors may:  
- Travel for **tourism**, leisure, or visiting family and friends.  
- Attend **business meetings, conferences, or short training** (if not employed in the UK).  
- Enroll in a **short study course** (up to 6 months).  
- Receive **private medical treatment** in the UK.  
- Participate in **academic exchanges** or **perform as an artist, musician, or athlete** for permitted short-term events.  

4. Financial and Documentation Requirements  
- Proof of sufficient **funds** to cover travel, accommodation, and living expenses.  
- A **valid return or onward travel booking** showing intent to leave the UK.  
- Evidence of **ties to the home country**, such as employment, property ownership, or family connections.  
- Supporting documentation may include:  
  • Bank statements or proof of income.  
  • Invitation letters (if visiting family or attending events).  
  • Accommodation confirmations or hotel reservations.  

5. Restrictions and Prohibited Activities  
Under this visa, applicants **cannot**:  
- Take up employment or self-employment in the UK.  
- Claim public funds or access state benefits.  
- Marry or register a civil partnership (a Marriage Visitor Visa is required for that purpose).  
- Undertake a long-term course of study.  
- Stay in the UK for more than 6 months at a time.  
- Switch to another visa category from within the UK.  

6. Application Process  
1. Apply online through the official **UK Government visa portal**.  
2. Pay the **visa application fee**.  
3. Book and attend an appointment at a **Visa Application Centre (VAC)** for biometrics.  
4. Submit supporting documents proving eligibility, purpose of visit, and financial stability.  
5. Await a decision from UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI).  

7. Validity and Duration  
- The Standard Visitor Visa is generally valid for **up to 6 months per visit**.  
- Long-term multiple-entry options (2, 5, or 10 years) are available for frequent travelers, but each stay cannot exceed 6 months.  

8. Ineligibility Conditions  
Applications may be refused if the applicant:  
- Fails to prove intent to leave the UK after the visit.  
- Does not demonstrate sufficient financial means.  
- Provides false or misleading documentation.  
- Has a history of visa overstays, immigration breaches, or criminal offenses.  
- Poses a risk to UK public security or policy.  

9. Legal References  
- **UK Immigration Rules Appendix V – Visitor**.  
- **Home Office Guidance: Visit Visa Policy (2024 Edition)**.  
- **Immigration Act 1971** and associated regulations.  

10. Notes  
- Visitors must not access **NHS services** except in emergencies.  
- Frequent visits may trigger additional scrutiny or refusal if seen as residence misuse.  
- Dependents must apply for separate visas if accompanying the main applicant.  
- The visa must be obtained **prior to travel** (for non-visa-exempt nationals).
