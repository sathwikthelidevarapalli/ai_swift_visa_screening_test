Source: https://www.gov.uk/graduate-visa
Country: United Kingdom
Visa Type: Graduate Visa

1. Overview
The Graduate Visa allows international students who have successfully completed an eligible course in the UK to stay and work, or look for work, after graduation.  
This visa provides an opportunity to gain work experience in the UK without the need for employer sponsorship and supports the transition to other long-term visa categories such as the Skilled Worker Visa.  

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- The applicant must be **physically present in the UK** at the time of application.  
- The applicant must **hold a valid Student visa or Tier 4 (General) Student visa** at the time of applying.  
- The applicant must have **successfully completed a UK bachelor’s degree, postgraduate degree, or other eligible qualification** from a recognized institution.  
- The **education provider must have reported to the Home Office** that the applicant has successfully completed their course of study.  
- The qualification must have been obtained from a **licensed Student Sponsor** with a track record of compliance.  

3. Eligible Qualifications
Applicants may qualify if they completed one of the following in the UK:  
- Bachelor’s degree (undergraduate).  
- Postgraduate degree (Master’s or PhD).  
- Other eligible professional or higher education qualifications recognized by the UK Home Office.  
Courses completed through **distance learning** or at **non-licensed institutions** do not qualify unless special COVID-related concessions apply.  

4. Duration and Conditions
- The Graduate Visa is typically granted for:  
  • **2 years** for bachelor’s or master’s degree graduates.  
  • **3 years** for PhD or other doctoral qualification holders.  
- The visa is **non-extendable**, but holders may switch to other work-related visas (such as the Skilled Worker or Global Talent Visa).  
- The visa allows unrestricted work, including self-employment, except as a professional sportsperson or coach.  

5. Financial and Health Requirements
- Applicants do **not need to show proof of funds** when applying, provided they meet maintenance requirements under their current visa.  
- Applicants must pay the **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)** to access UK healthcare during their stay.  
- No additional English language test is required, as eligibility is based on completing a UK qualification taught in English.  

6. Application Process
1. Apply **online** through the official UK Home Office website.  
2. Provide the **Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) number** used for the Student Visa.  
3. Upload documents proving completion of the course (degree certificate, transcript, or completion letter).  
4. Pay the application fee and Immigration Health Surcharge.  
5. Biometric data submission may be required through the UK Immigration: ID Check app or at a visa application center.  

7. Required Documentation
- Valid passport or travel document.  
- Proof of current visa status (Student or Tier 4).  
- Degree certificate, official transcript, or confirmation of course completion.  
- CAS reference number from the previous Student visa.  
- Payment confirmation of visa fee and Immigration Health Surcharge.  

8. Ineligibility Conditions
The application may be refused if the applicant:  
- Has not successfully completed an eligible course.  
- Completed the course at a non-licensed institution or under a revoked sponsorship.  
- Is applying from outside the UK.  
- Has overstayed or breached visa conditions.  
- Provides false or incomplete documentation.  

9. Benefits and Opportunities
- Freedom to work for any employer in the UK, including part-time, full-time, or self-employed roles.  
- No job offer or minimum salary requirement.  
- Ability to gain UK work experience to strengthen future visa or settlement applications.  
- Opportunity to switch to long-term visa categories (e.g., Skilled Worker, Global Talent, Innovator Founder).  

10. Legal References
- **UK Immigration Rules Appendix Graduate**.  
- **Home Office Graduate Route Guidance (2023)**.  
- **Immigration Act 1971** and related statutory instruments governing post-study work routes.  

11. Notes
- The Graduate Visa **cannot be extended** but provides a pathway to permanent residence through subsequent visas.  
- Dependents previously on the Student visa may also extend their stay under the Graduate route, if eligible.  
- Applicants are advised to apply **before their current visa expires** to maintain lawful status.  
- Time spent under the Graduate Visa does **not count toward settlement eligibility** directly but contributes to residence history for future applications.
