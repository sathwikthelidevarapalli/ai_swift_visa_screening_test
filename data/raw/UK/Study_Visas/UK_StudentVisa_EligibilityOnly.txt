Source: https://www.gov.uk/student-visa
Country: United Kingdom
Visa Type: Student Visa

1. Overview
The UK Student Visa allows international students aged 16 and above to study full-time at approved higher education institutions, colleges, or schools in the United Kingdom.  
It replaces the former Tier 4 (General) Student Visa and enables students to pursue academic, professional, or vocational qualifications while maintaining lawful residence in the UK.  

This visa supports higher education, postgraduate study, and specialized programs at licensed student sponsors recognized by UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI).

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- The applicant must be **16 years of age or older** at the time of application.  
- The applicant must have an **unconditional offer of a place** on a course from a **licensed student sponsor**.  
- The applicant must have received a **Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS)** from the education provider.  
- The applicant must have **sufficient financial resources** to cover tuition fees and living expenses for the duration of the course.  
- The applicant must be able to **speak, read, write, and understand English** at the level required by the course and institution.  
- Applicants aged **16 or 17** must provide **parental or guardian consent** to study in the UK.  
- The applicant must meet all **immigration and health requirements**, including medical checks and biometric verification.  

3. Financial Requirements
- The applicant must show proof of funds to cover:  
  • **Tuition fees** for the first year of study, and  
  • **Living costs** (minimum amount set by UKVI depending on the city of study).  
- Financial evidence can include:  
  • Bank statements or financial sponsorship letters, and  
  • Proof of student loans or scholarships.  
- Funds must be available for at least **28 consecutive days** before the application date.  

4. English Language Requirements
- The applicant must meet the **minimum English language standard** based on the level of study:  
  • **RQF Level 6 and above (undergraduate or higher)** – B2 level on the CEFR scale.  
  • **Below RQF Level 6 (college level)** – B1 level on the CEFR scale.  
- Proof can be provided through:  
  • A **Secure English Language Test (SELT)** approved by UKVI, or  
  • A degree taught in English or previous study in a majority English-speaking country.  

5. Course and Sponsor Requirements
- The course must be offered by a **licensed student sponsor** listed on the official **Register of Licensed Sponsors**.  
- Eligible courses include:  
  • Full-time degrees (undergraduate, master’s, or doctoral programs).  
  • Foundation or pre-sessional English courses.  
  • Further or higher education diplomas.  
- The sponsor must issue a **Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS)** with a unique reference number included in the visa application.  

6. Health and Immigration Requirements
- Applicants must pay the **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)** to access healthcare through the **National Health Service (NHS)**.  
- Applicants must provide a **valid passport** and undergo **biometric enrollment** at a visa application center.  
- Some applicants may be required to complete a **tuberculosis (TB) test**, depending on their country of residence.  

7. Application Process
1. Apply online through the official **UK Government Visa Application Portal**.  
2. Pay the visa fee and Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS).  
3. Submit biometric information and supporting documents.  
4. Receive a decision and collect the visa vignette (entry clearance) before travel.  
5. Upon arrival, collect the **Biometric Residence Permit (BRP)** or digital status confirmation.  

8. Validity and Conditions
- The visa duration typically covers the **length of the course plus 4 months** after completion.  
- Students may **work part-time** during term (up to 20 hours per week) and **full-time** during holidays, depending on the level of study and sponsor type.  
- Dependents (spouse and children) may accompany postgraduate students under specific conditions.  
- Visa extensions are permitted for continuing study or switching to another eligible visa category (e.g., Graduate Visa).  

9. Ineligibility Conditions
The application may be refused if the applicant:  
- Fails to prove sufficient financial resources or course enrollment.  
- Applies to an unlicensed or revoked institution.  
- Does not meet English language or academic entry requirements.  
- Provides incomplete, false, or misleading information.  
- Has a history of visa overstays or breaches of UK immigration laws.  

10. Legal References
- **UK Immigration Rules Appendix Student**.  
- **Immigration Act 1971** and related statutory instruments.  
- **Home Office Guidance – Student Route (2024 Edition)**.  

11. Notes
- Students must apply for the visa **no more than 6 months before the course start date**.  
- Dependents are only allowed if the student is enrolled in a **postgraduate course (RQF Level 7 or above)** lasting 9 months or longer.  
- Students must register with their education provider after arrival and maintain attendance to comply with visa conditions.  
- Time spent under this visa may contribute toward future **Skilled Worker** or **Graduate Visa** applications but not direct settlement eligibility.
