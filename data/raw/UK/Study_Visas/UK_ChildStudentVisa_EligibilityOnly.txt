Source: https://www.gov.uk/child-study-visa/eligibility
Country: United Kingdom
Visa Type: Child Student Visa

1. Overview
The Child Student Visa allows children aged 4 to 17 years to study full-time at an independent, fee-paying school in the United Kingdom.  
It enables young students to receive quality education in a safe and regulated environment, provided they meet financial, parental consent, and institutional requirements.  
This visa replaces the former Tier 4 (Child) Student route.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- The applicant must be **between 4 and 17 years old** at the time of application.  
- Applicants aged **16 or 17** may also be eligible for the **Student Visa (adult route)** if pursuing higher-level studies.  
- The applicant must have an **unconditional offer** of a place at an **independent school** holding a **valid student sponsor licence** issued by the UK Home Office.  
- The applicant must **not intend to study at a state-funded school** or academy.  

3. Financial Requirements
- The applicant must demonstrate sufficient funds to cover:  
  • **Tuition fees** for the duration of the study program, and  
  • **Living expenses** (boarding or accommodation) while in the UK.  
- The financial evidence must cover the **first academic year** or **nine months** of the course, whichever is shorter.  
- The funds must be held in a regulated financial institution and accessible to the applicant or their parent/guardian.  

4. Parental Consent and Guardianship
- Applicants under 18 must provide **written consent** from both parents (or legal guardian) confirming:  
  • Permission to travel to the UK.  
  • Consent for study arrangements.  
  • Approval of living arrangements and care in the UK.  
- Children aged **under 12 years** must have appropriate **care arrangements** in the UK, such as staying with a parent, legal guardian, or approved private foster carer.  

5. Accommodation and Care Arrangements
- Applicants must provide details of **living arrangements** while studying, including the name, address, and relationship of the guardian or host in the UK.  
- If the child will live with a **parent holding a Parent of a Child Student Visa**, proof of relationship and valid immigration status must be provided.  
- Schools must confirm that **suitable accommodation and welfare provisions** are in place for all international students under 18.  

6. Language and Academic Requirements
- For primary and secondary education, **formal English proficiency testing is not mandatory**, but schools may conduct internal assessments.  
- For older students (16–17 years), schools may set academic or English proficiency standards depending on the program.  

7. Health and Insurance Requirements
- The applicant must pay the **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)** as part of the visa process.  
- The applicant must be free of serious communicable diseases and comply with UK health and vaccination regulations.  

8. Validity and Duration
- The visa duration depends on the applicant’s age and length of course:  
  • Up to **6 years** for children aged 4–15.  
  • Up to **3 years** for children aged 16–17.  
- The visa allows multiple entries and may be extended if the child continues studying at a qualifying institution.  

9. Ineligibility Conditions
An application may be refused if the applicant:  
- Fails to demonstrate adequate funding or parental consent.  
- Applies to study at a non-licensed or state-funded institution.  
- Provides false or incomplete information.  
- Has a history of immigration breaches or criminal conduct.  

10. Legal References
- **UK Immigration Rules Part 6A – Child Student Route**.  
- **Appendix Child Student of the Immigration Rules**.  
- **UK Home Office Guidance: Child Student Visa Policy**.  

11. Notes
- Dependents are **not permitted** on a Child Student Visa.  
- A **Parent of a Child Student Visa** is available separately for one parent of a child under 12 years studying in the UK.  
- Applicants must apply for the visa **outside the UK** before traveling.  
- Schools may assist with visa documentation as licensed sponsors.  
- The visa does not lead directly to settlement, but long-term study may support future immigration applications.
