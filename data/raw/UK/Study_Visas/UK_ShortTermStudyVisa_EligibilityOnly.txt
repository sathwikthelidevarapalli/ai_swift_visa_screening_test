Source: https://www.gov.uk/visa-to-study-english
Country: United Kingdom
Visa Type: Short-term Study Visa (English Language Course)

1. Overview
The Short-term Study Visa allows international students to visit the United Kingdom to study an English language course lasting more than 6 months and up to 11 months.  
It is designed for learners who wish to improve their English proficiency at accredited institutions without enrolling in a long-term academic or degree program.  
This visa is temporary and does not provide work rights, settlement options, or the ability to extend or switch to another visa type within the UK.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- The applicant must be **accepted onto an English language course** at an **approved UK education provider**.  
- The course must be **longer than 6 months and not exceed 11 months** in duration.  
- The applicant must be **aged 16 or above** at the time of application.  
- The primary purpose of travel must be **studying English**; no other types of study are permitted under this visa.  
- The applicant must **apply from outside the UK** and cannot switch to or extend another visa under this route.  

3. Financial Requirements
- The applicant must demonstrate **sufficient funds** to cover:  
  • Tuition fees for the entire duration of the course, and  
  • Living expenses, accommodation, and return travel costs.  
- Funds must be held in a regulated financial institution and available at the time of application.  
- Financial support can be shown through bank statements, sponsor letters, or official financial guarantees.  

4. Education and Sponsorship
- The English language course must be offered by an **accredited institution** approved by the UK Home Office or listed on the **Register of Licensed Sponsors**.  
- A valid **confirmation of acceptance or enrollment letter** must be provided, detailing:  
  • Course name and duration.  
  • Institution details and contact information.  
  • Tuition fees and payment arrangements.  

5. Health and Insurance Requirements
- Applicants staying for more than 6 months must pay the **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)** to access the National Health Service (NHS).  
- Applicants must be free of serious communicable diseases and may be required to undergo a **tuberculosis (TB) test**, depending on country of residence.  

6. Application Process
1. Apply **online** via the official UK Home Office website before traveling to the UK.  
2. Submit biometric information (fingerprints and photograph) at a visa application center.  
3. Provide proof of enrollment, financial documents, and travel details.  
4. Wait for visa approval before making travel arrangements.  
5. Collect the visa vignette from the passport and enter the UK within the approved travel period.  

7. Required Documentation
- Valid passport or travel document.  
- Acceptance or enrollment letter from the approved institution.  
- Proof of sufficient funds (bank statements, sponsor letters).  
- Evidence of accommodation and travel plans.  
- Proof of English course duration (between 6 and 11 months).  
- Tuberculosis test results (if required).  
- Payment receipt for visa fee and Immigration Health Surcharge.  

8. Conditions and Restrictions
- Visa holders **cannot work**, **extend their stay**, or **switch to another visa** within the UK.  
- Dependents (family members) are **not permitted** to accompany or join under this visa category.  
- The visa only allows study at the institution and course specified in the application.  
- The applicant must leave the UK at the end of the visa validity period.  

9. Ineligibility Conditions
The application may be refused if the applicant:  
- Does not provide proof of course acceptance at an approved institution.  
- Fails to show sufficient funds or credible travel plans.  
- Intends to work or study in programs outside English language instruction.  
- Has overstayed a previous UK visa or violated immigration laws.  
- Submits incomplete or misleading documentation.  

10. Legal References
- **UK Immigration Rules – Appendix ST: Short-term Student (English Language)**.  
- **Immigration Act 1971** and subsequent amendments governing temporary study routes.  
- **Home Office Guidance – Short-term Study Visa Policy (2024 Edition)**.  

11. Notes
- Applicants should apply **no earlier than 6 months before** the intended start date of their course.  
- The visa typically allows entry up to **30 days before** the course begins.  
- Time spent under this visa does **not count toward settlement** or future work visa eligibility.  
- Institutions may issue supporting documentation for visa renewal outside the UK, if further English study is required.
