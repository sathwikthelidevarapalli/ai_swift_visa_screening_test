Source: https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/temporary-workers/h-3-nonimmigrant-trainee-or-special-education-exchange-visitor
Country: United States
Visa Type: H-3 Nonimmigrant Trainee or Special Education Exchange Visitor

1. Overview  
The H-3 nonimmigrant visa category allows foreign nationals to come temporarily to the United States either as trainees or as participants in a special education exchange visitor program. The purpose of this visa is to provide individuals with structured training or experience not available in their home countries, which they can use to enhance their professional development upon return.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements  
- The applicant must be invited by a U.S. employer, organization, or institution to receive training in a field that is not available in their home country.  
- The training cannot be primarily for productive employment and should only include such work when incidental and necessary to the training.  
- The applicant cannot be placed in a position that is part of the normal operations of the U.S. employer or where U.S. workers are regularly employed.  
- The proposed training program must be formal, structured, and described in detail, including objectives, supervision, and evaluation methods.  
- The U.S. employer or sponsor must demonstrate that the training will benefit the applicant in pursuing a career outside the United States.  
- The applicant must have the background and qualifications appropriate for the training opportunity offered.  
- For “special education exchange visitor” category, the applicant must be coming to participate in a training program focused on educating children with physical, mental, or emotional disabilities.

3. Specific Eligibility – Special Education Exchange Visitor  
- The applicant must either have completed or be pursuing a bachelor’s or higher degree in special education, **or** have extensive practical experience in the field.  
- The training program must take place at a facility with professionally trained staff and must provide hands-on experience under qualified supervision.  
- The number of special education exchange visitors accepted by a U.S. organization is limited to **50 individuals per year**.  
- The program should enhance the participant’s expertise and capacity to contribute to special education programs in their home country.

4. Validity Period and Extensions  
- The H-3 trainee visa is generally valid for the length of the training program, up to a maximum of **2 years**.  
- For the special education exchange visitor category, the maximum period of stay is **18 months**.  
- Extensions beyond these periods are not typically granted.  
- After completing the training, the participant must return to their home country and apply the acquired skills abroad.

5. Employer/Sponsor Obligations  
- Must submit a detailed training plan outlining:  
  • The structure and duration of the program.  
  • The type of training and supervision to be provided.  
  • The number of hours devoted to classroom instruction and on-the-job training.  
  • How the program will benefit the trainee’s career outside the United States.  
- Must certify that the training is not available in the applicant’s home country.  
- Must ensure the program does not displace or replace U.S. workers and does not constitute ordinary employment.  
- Must comply with USCIS and Department of Labor (DOL) standards regarding foreign trainees.

6. Application Steps  
1. U.S. employer or sponsor prepares a detailed training plan and gathers supporting documentation.  
2. Employer files **Form I-129, Petition for a Nonimmigrant Worker**, with USCIS, along with the training description and proof of eligibility.  
3. Upon approval, USCIS issues a Form I-797 Notice of Action.  
4. The applicant applies for an H-3 visa at a U.S. embassy or consulate abroad.  
5. After visa issuance, the trainee may enter the U.S. to begin the approved program.  

7. Key Documents Required  
- Form I-129, Petition for a Nonimmigrant Worker.  
- Detailed description of the training program (curriculum, duration, supervision).  
- Evidence that similar training is not available in the applicant’s home country.  
- Proof of applicant’s educational background or experience relevant to the program.  
- Documentation showing how the training benefits the applicant’s future career abroad.  
- Supporting materials for special education exchange programs, including facility credentials and staff qualifications.  

8. Common Reasons for Ineligibility  
- Training program involves normal productive employment or lacks clear educational purpose.  
- Employer fails to show that similar training is unavailable in the applicant’s home country.  
- Application lacks a comprehensive and structured training plan.  
- Applicant lacks relevant qualifications or background for the proposed training.  
- The proposed activities appear to replace a U.S. worker.  
- Submission of false or incomplete documentation.  

9. Legal References  
- Immigration and Nationality Act (INA) §101(a)(15)(H)(iii).  
- 8 CFR 214.2(h)(7) — Regulations for H-3 Trainee and Special Education Programs.  
- USCIS Policy Manual, Volume 2, Part H — Temporary Workers.

10. Notes  
- H-3 trainees are not authorized to engage in productive employment outside their training program.  
- Dependents (spouse and unmarried children under 21) may apply for H-4 visa status but cannot work in the U.S.  
- H-3 status cannot be used to gain practical work experience that could be obtained abroad.  
- Upon completion, participants are expected to return to their home countries and apply the acquired knowledge professionally.
