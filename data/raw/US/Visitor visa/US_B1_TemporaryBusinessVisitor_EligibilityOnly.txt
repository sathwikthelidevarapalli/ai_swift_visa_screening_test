Country: United States
Visa Type: B-1 Temporary Business Visitor

1. Overview
The B-1 Temporary Business Visitor visa allows foreign nationals to enter the United States temporarily for legitimate business activities that do not involve gainful employment or labor for hire. Common permissible activities include attending business meetings, conferences, conventions, negotiations, or performing independent research on behalf of a foreign employer.

B-1 visa holders are considered temporary visitors for business purposes and must not engage in productive work for a U.S. employer or receive payment from a U.S. source other than for incidental expenses.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- The applicant’s trip must be for **legitimate business purposes** such as attending meetings, conferences, consultations, or contract negotiations.
- The applicant must **intend to remain in the United States only for a specific and limited period** consistent with the purpose of the visit.
- The applicant must demonstrate **sufficient financial resources** to cover travel, accommodation, and living expenses during the stay without seeking U.S. employment.
- The applicant must **maintain a residence outside the United States** and have no intent to abandon it.
- The applicant must **demonstrate strong ties** (such as family, employment, or property) to their home country ensuring return after the visit.
- The applicant must be **admissible under U.S. immigration law**, with no grounds of ineligibility such as prior immigration violations or criminal history.

3. Permissible Business Activities
- Consulting with business associates or clients.  
- Attending scientific, educational, professional, or business conventions or conferences.  
- Settling an estate or negotiating a contract.  
- Participating in short-term training or independent professional research.  
- Representing a foreign employer for business purposes.  

**Note:** B-1 visitors cannot engage in local employment or receive a salary from a U.S. source.

4. Duration and Extensions
- The initial stay is generally granted for **up to six months**.  
- An extension of stay may be requested for up to another six months if justified.  
- The applicant must demonstrate continued eligibility for temporary stay and valid purpose when applying for an extension.

5. Financial and Documentation Requirements
- Proof of return travel arrangements (such as round-trip tickets).  
- Evidence of financial capacity (bank statements, company sponsorship letters, or expense coverage statements).  
- Invitation letter or conference registration from the U.S. entity (if applicable).  
- Valid passport and proof of nonimmigrant intent.

6. Common Reasons for Ineligibility
- Attempting to engage in unauthorized employment or paid work.  
- Insufficient evidence of temporary intent or ties abroad.  
- Lack of funds to support stay in the United States.  
- Failure to demonstrate a legitimate business purpose.  
- Misrepresentation or prior immigration violations.

8. Notes
- B-1 visa holders are not permitted to study, work, or be employed by a U.S. entity.  
- Reimbursement for incidental expenses (e.g., travel, lodging, meals) is permitted if directly related to business activities.  
- Dependents are not eligible for derivative B-1 status; each individual must qualify independently.  
- B-1 visa holders may be eligible for entry under the **Visa Waiver Program (VWP)** if they hold passports from designated countries.
