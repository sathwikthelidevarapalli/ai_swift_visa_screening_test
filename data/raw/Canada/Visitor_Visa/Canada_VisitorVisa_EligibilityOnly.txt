Country: Canada
Visa Type: Visitor Visa (Temporary Resident Visa)

1. Overview
The Visitor Visa, also known as the Temporary Resident Visa (TRV), allows foreign nationals to travel to Canada for a temporary stay for tourism, visiting family or friends, or attending business meetings or short-term courses.  
It is typically issued for a period of up to six months and may be for single or multiple entries depending on the applicant’s purpose and travel history.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- Must hold a **valid travel document**, such as a passport.  
- Must be in **good health**; a medical examination may be required for certain applicants or extended stays.  
- Must have **no criminal record** or immigration-related convictions. Applicants may need to provide a police clearance certificate.  
- Must **demonstrate strong ties** to the home country, such as employment, property ownership, financial assets, or close family relationships, to prove intent to return.  
- Must show **intent to leave Canada** at the end of the authorized stay.  
- Must have **sufficient financial resources** to cover the cost of travel, accommodation, and living expenses during the stay in Canada. The required amount varies based on trip duration, purpose, and living arrangements.  
- Must not pose a security or health risk to Canada.  

3. Additional Conditions
- Applicants from visa-required countries must obtain a TRV before travel.  
- Some applicants may need to provide **biometrics** (fingerprints and photo).  
- A **letter of invitation** may be required if visiting family or attending an event in Canada.  
- Individuals from visa-exempt countries may instead need an **Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA)** if arriving by air.  
- Business visitors must not engage in employment within Canada unless authorized by a separate work permit.  

4. Validity and Duration
- A Visitor Visa is generally valid for up to **10 years or until the passport expires**, whichever comes first.  
- Each entry allows the holder to **stay for up to six months**, unless a shorter period is specified by a border services officer.  
- Extensions can be applied for from within Canada using **Form IMM 5708** before the current status expires.  

5. Application Steps
1. Complete the **online application** for a Visitor Visa through the IRCC website.  
2. Gather required documents, including proof of financial support, travel history, and ties to home country.  
3. Pay the **application and biometrics fees**.  
4. Submit **biometrics** (if required) at a Visa Application Centre (VAC).  
5. Wait for processing and decision by IRCC.  
6. Upon approval, receive visa stamping in passport before travel.  

6. Required Documentation
- Valid passport or travel document.  
- Digital photograph and biometrics (if required).  
- Proof of financial support (bank statements, employment letter, pay slips, etc.).  
- Proof of ties to home country (employment, family, property).  
- Purpose of visit (itinerary, invitation letter, travel history).  
- Police clearance certificate (if requested).  
- Medical examination report (if applicable).  

7. Ineligibility Conditions
Applicants may be deemed ineligible if they:  
- Have a criminal record or history of immigration violations.  
- Fail to prove sufficient financial means or intent to return home.  
- Pose a security, health, or safety risk.  
- Submit false or incomplete documentation.  
- Are inadmissible under the **Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA)**.  

8. Legal References
- **Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA)**, Sections 11, 16, and 20 — Entry requirements for temporary residents.  
- **Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations (IRPR)**, Part 9 — Temporary Residents.  
- **Ministerial Instructions (IRCC)** — Visitor Visa and eTA requirements.  

9. Notes
- Visitor Visa holders are **not authorized to work or study** in Canada without the appropriate permit.  
- An extension must be applied for before the expiry of current authorized stay.  
- The **Super Visa** program exists separately for long-term visits by parents or grandparents of Canadian citizens or permanent residents.  
- Biometrics remain valid for **10 years** and can be reused for future applications.
