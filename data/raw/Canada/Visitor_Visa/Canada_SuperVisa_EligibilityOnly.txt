Country: Canada
Visa Type: Super Visa (Parents & Grandparents Visitor Visa)

1. Overview
The Super Visa is a long-term multiple-entry visa that allows parents and grandparents of Canadian citizens, permanent residents, or registered Indians to visit Canada for extended periods.  
Unlike a regular visitor visa, the Super Visa permits stays of up to **5 years per entry**, with the option for extensions.  
It is designed to facilitate family reunification while ensuring visitors have sufficient financial and medical support during their stay.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- The applicant must be a **parent or grandparent** of a Canadian citizen, permanent resident, or registered Indian.  
- The applicant’s **child or grandchild in Canada** must:  
  • Be **at least 18 years old**.  
  • **Reside in Canada** at the time of application.  
  • Meet or exceed the **Minimum Necessary Income (MNI)** threshold for their family size.  
- The host (child or grandchild) must provide a **signed invitation letter** that includes:  
  • A written commitment to financially support the applicant during their stay.  
  • Details of household size and proof of income.  
  • Copy of Canadian citizenship or permanent resident document.  
- The applicant must apply from **outside Canada** and obtain a **visa or electronic travel authorization (eTA)** before entry.  
- The applicant must **be admissible to Canada**, which includes passing medical, criminal, and background checks.  

3. Medical and Insurance Requirements
- The applicant must complete an **immigration medical examination** by a panel physician approved by IRCC.  
- The applicant must purchase **private medical insurance** from a Canadian or approved foreign insurer that:  
  • Is **valid for at least one year** from the date of entry.  
  • Covers **health care, hospitalization, and repatriation**.  
  • Provides **a minimum coverage of CAD $100,000**.  
- Proof of paid medical insurance must be included in the application.

4. Financial Support Requirements
- The Canadian child or grandchild must demonstrate that they meet the **Minimum Necessary Income (MNI)** based on family size, as defined by **Low Income Cut-Off (LICO)** guidelines.  
- Acceptable proof of income includes:  
  • Notice of Assessment (NOA) or T4/T1 tax documents.  
  • Employment Insurance stubs or pay slips.  
  • Letter from employer stating salary and employment duration.  
  • Bank statements.  
- The commitment letter and financial proof together ensure that visitors will not rely on public funds during their stay.

5. Validity and Duration
- The Super Visa is a **multiple-entry visa** that may be valid for up to **10 years**.  
- Each entry to Canada allows the holder to **stay up to 5 years** without the need to renew visitor status.  
- Extensions can be requested from within Canada if the visitor wishes to remain longer.

6. Application Process
1. Gather necessary documents, including proof of relationship (birth certificates), invitation letter, and medical insurance.  
2. Apply **online** or at a **Visa Application Centre (VAC)** outside Canada.  
3. Provide **biometrics** (fingerprints and photo), if required.  
4. Complete a **medical examination** by an IRCC-approved panel physician.  
5. Submit the application with all supporting evidence and pay required fees.  
6. Wait for IRCC decision and visa issuance before travel.

7. Required Documentation
- Valid passport.  
- Proof of relationship to the Canadian host (birth or adoption certificate).  
- Invitation letter from child or grandchild in Canada.  
- Proof of the host’s income and employment.  
- Evidence of medical insurance coverage for one year.  
- Proof of completed immigration medical exam.  
- Recent photographs (per IRCC specifications).  
- Application forms (IMM 5257, IMM 5645, etc.).  

8. Ineligibility Conditions
Applicants may be found ineligible if they:  
- Do not meet financial or medical insurance requirements.  
- Fail to provide sufficient proof of relationship or family ties.  
- Have previous immigration violations or criminal records.  
- Are medically inadmissible under IRPA Section 38(1).  
- Cannot satisfy the visa officer of their intent to leave Canada at the end of their authorized stay.

9. Legal References
- Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA), Sections 11 and 39–42 — Requirements for temporary residents and admissibility.  
- Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations (IRPR), Part 9 — Temporary Residents.  
- Ministerial Instructions on Super Visa Program (IRCC Operational Bulletin 490).  

10. Notes
- The Super Visa is distinct from the **Parent and Grandparent Sponsorship Program (PR pathway)**.  
- Visitors must maintain **valid medical insurance** throughout their stay.  
- Having a Super Visa does **not grant permanent resident status**.  
- Holders may travel multiple times during the visa’s validity period without reapplying.  
- Visitors must comply with all conditions of temporary resident status while in Canada.
