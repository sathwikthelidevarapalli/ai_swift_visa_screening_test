Country: Canada
Permit Type: Work Permit (Temporary Work Authorization)

1. Overview
A Work Permit allows foreign nationals to work temporarily in Canada under specific conditions approved by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC).  
It may be issued under the **Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP)**, which requires a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA), or under **LMIA-exempt categories** such as international agreements, intra-company transfers, or reciprocal employment.  
The permit does not provide permanent residence status but may serve as a pathway to it through various immigration programs.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- Must meet general eligibility conditions to work in Canada:  
  • Obey Canadian law and have no criminal record (police certificate may be required).  
  • Be in good health; medical examination may be required for certain occupations or long-term stays.  
  • Demonstrate intent to leave Canada upon expiration of work authorization.  
  • Have sufficient financial means to support oneself and dependents during the stay and return journey.  
- Must **not plan to work for an employer** who:  
  • Is listed as **ineligible** on the Government of Canada’s employer blacklist.  
  • Regularly offers services such as strip-tease, erotic dance, escort services, or erotic massages.  

3. Eligibility for Applicants Inside Canada
Applicants applying for a work permit **from within Canada** must meet at least one of the following criteria:
- Hold a **valid study or work permit**.  
- Be the **spouse, common-law partner, or dependent child** of someone holding a valid study or work permit.  
- Be eligible for a **Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)** and hold a valid study permit.  
- Hold a **Temporary Resident Permit (TRP)** valid for at least six months.  
- Have applied for **permanent residence** in certain eligible classes and are awaiting a decision.  
- Have **claimed refugee protection**, been recognized as a **protected person**, or are eligible for work authorization under humanitarian programs.  
- Be eligible under **international agreements**, such as:  
  • **Canada–United States–Mexico Agreement (CUSMA/USMCA)** — Traders, Investors, Intra-company Transferees.  
  • **General Agreement on Trade in Services (GATS)** or other international mobility programs.

4. Eligibility for Applicants Outside Canada
Applicants outside Canada may apply for a work permit if they:
- Have received a **valid job offer** from a Canadian employer supported by a positive **Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA)** (if required).  
- Qualify for an **LMIA-exempt work permit** under categories such as International Mobility Program (IMP), open work permits, or agreements like IEC.  
- Meet all **admissibility requirements**, including background checks, medical exams, and financial sufficiency.  
- Intend to perform authorized work and depart after the completion of employment.

5. Eligibility for Applicants at a Port of Entry (POE)
Applicants may apply directly at a **port of entry (airport or land border)** if they:
- Are from a **visa-exempt country**, or  
- Hold an **Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA)** or other travel authorization permitting entry, and  
- Possess all required documentation, such as an LMIA approval, job offer letter, and proof of qualifications.

6. Medical and Security Requirements
- Must undergo a **medical examination** if the applicant:  
  • Intends to work in healthcare, childcare, or primary/secondary education.  
  • Has lived or traveled in certain designated countries within the last year.  
- Must provide **police clearance certificates** from all countries lived in for 6+ months since age 18.  
- Must not pose a security, health, or criminal risk to Canada.

7. Duration and Conditions
- Work permits are typically issued for the **length of the employment offer**, up to a maximum of **4 years**, depending on program type.  
- Extensions may be possible if the applicant continues to meet program requirements.  
- Permit holders must comply with all conditions stated, including occupation, employer, and location restrictions.

8. Required Documentation
- Completed work permit application forms (IMM 1295, IMM 5707, etc.).  
- Valid passport or travel document.  
- Offer of employment or LMIA approval (if applicable).  
- Proof of education, training, or experience required for the job.  
- Medical and police certificates (if requested).  
- Proof of financial support and intent to return home.  
- Biometrics (photo and fingerprints).  

9. Ineligibility Conditions
Applicants may be found ineligible if they:
- Work for an ineligible employer or prohibited business sector.  
- Fail to meet health or security standards.  
- Provide false or incomplete information.  
- Have overstayed or violated immigration status in Canada.  
- Cannot demonstrate sufficient financial or personal ties to their home country.

10. Legal References
- **Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA)**, Sections 11, 20, and 200–205 — Requirements for temporary residents and work authorization.  
- **Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations (IRPR)**, Part 11 — Work Permits.  
- **IRCC Program Delivery Instructions** — Work Permit Eligibility and LMIA-Exempt Categories.  

11. Notes
- A **work permit is not a visa**; applicants may still require a TRV or eTA to enter Canada.  
- Holders of valid work permits may be eligible for **permanent residence** through programs such as Express Entry, Provincial Nominee Program (PNP), or the Canadian Experience Class (CEC).  
- Spouses or common-law partners of work permit holders may qualify for an **open work permit**.  
- Work permit extensions must be applied for **before current status expires** to maintain legal residence.
