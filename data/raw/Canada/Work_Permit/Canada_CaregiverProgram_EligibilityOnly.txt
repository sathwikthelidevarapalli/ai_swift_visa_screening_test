Source: https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/caregivers.html
Country: Canada
Program Type: Caregiver Immigration / Work Permit Pathway

1. Overview
The Caregiver Immigration Pathway allows qualified foreign nationals to come to Canada to work in eligible home care occupations and eventually apply for permanent residence.  
It consists of two main programs:  
- **Home Child Care Provider Pilot**  
- **Home Support Worker Pilot**  
These programs are designed to help Canadian families hire caregivers to provide essential support while offering workers a direct route to permanent residency after meeting certain criteria.

2. Basic Eligibility Requirements
- Must **intend to live and work in Canada** (outside the province of Quebec) as a **home child care provider** or **home support worker**.  
- Must possess at least a **Canadian high school diploma** or an **equivalent foreign educational credential** assessed by an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA).  
- Must meet the **minimum language proficiency level**:  
  • **Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB) 5** in English, or **Niveaux de compétence linguistique canadiens (NCLC) 5** in French.  
- Must have **relevant work experience or training** in caregiving that aligns with the job duties of National Occupational Classification (NOC) codes:  
  • NOC 44100 – Home Child Care Provider  
  • NOC 44101 – Home Support Worker  
- Must hold a **valid, full-time job offer** from a Canadian employer in one of the eligible caregiving occupations.  
- Must be **admissible to Canada** under immigration law (no criminal record, security issues, or medical inadmissibility).  

3. Work Experience Requirement
- Applicants must demonstrate at least **24 months (2 years) of full-time work experience** in Canada in an eligible occupation within the past 36 months.  
- Experience must be **authorized work** under a valid work permit and directly related to the caregiving duties listed in the NOC.  
- Unpaid volunteer work does not count toward this requirement.  

4. Education Requirement
- Must have completed a **minimum of a high school diploma** (secondary education).  
- Foreign credentials must be verified through an **Educational Credential Assessment (ECA)** from an approved organization such as WES, ICAS, or CES.  

5. Language Requirement
- Must achieve a minimum of **CLB/NCLC Level 5** in all four language abilities: reading, writing, speaking, and listening.  
- Proof of results must come from an approved test provider, such as IELTS (General Training) or CELPIP (General).  

6. Job Offer Criteria
- Must have a **genuine and valid job offer** for a **full-time, non-seasonal** position in Canada.  
- The offer must:  
  • Be from a single employer (not a staffing agency).  
  • Include caregiving duties for children, seniors, or persons with disabilities in a private home setting.  
  • Not require the caregiver to live in the employer’s home (optional accommodation).  
  • Comply with provincial or territorial employment laws and wage standards.  

7. Admissibility and Background Checks
- Applicants must pass:  
  • **Criminal background checks** (police clearance).  
  • **Medical examination** confirming good health.  
  • **Security and immigration history review** to ensure compliance with prior visa conditions.  

8. Pathway to Permanent Residence
- After obtaining at least **24 months of eligible work experience**, caregivers may apply for **permanent residence** under the same pilot stream.  
- Applicants may include family members (spouse and dependent children) in their permanent residence application.  
- While working in Canada, eligible family members may apply for open work or study permits.  

9. Required Documentation
- Valid passport and work permit application forms.  
- Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) report.  
- Proof of language test results (IELTS, CELPIP, TEF, or TCF).  
- Proof of job offer and employment contract.  
- Police clearance certificates and medical exam results.  
- Proof of work experience (reference letters, pay slips, employment contracts).  
- Relationship documents for accompanying family members (if applicable).  

10. Ineligibility Conditions
Applicants are ineligible if they:  
- Apply for employment in the province of Quebec.  
- Fail to meet education or language test requirements.  
- Have inadmissibility issues (criminal, medical, or misrepresentation).  
- Provide fraudulent documents or job offers.  
- Work in occupations outside the caregiving NOC codes (44100 or 44101).  

11. Legal References
- **Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA)**, Sections 11 and 12 — Requirements for admission and permanent residence.  
- **Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations (IRPR)**, Division 6 — Economic Classes.  
- **IRCC Program Delivery Instructions** — Caregiver Pilots (Home Child Care Provider and Home Support Worker).  

12. Notes
- Each pilot program admits up to **5,500 principal applicants annually**.  
- Caregivers and their families may transition to permanent residence once eligibility requirements are met.  
- Applicants outside Canada may apply directly for a combined work permit and PR eligibility assessment.  
- The pilots are currently open for applications until **June 2026**, subject to extension or replacement by a new caregiver program.
