 Overview The Skilled Worker Visa (National Visa / Residence Permit) allows qualified non-EU nationals to live and work in Germany in a profession that matches their recognized qualifications
 Basic Eligibility Requirements - The applicant must hold a **recognized foreign vocational qualification** or **university degree** equivalent to a German qualification
 - The applicant must have a **valid job offer or employment contract** in Germany that corresponds to their professional qualifications
 - The applicant must meet all **legal and professional requirements** applicable to the intended occupation (such as licenses or registrations for regulated professions)
 Qualification and Recognition Requirements - The applicant’s educational or professional qualification must be **formally recognized in Germany**
 - Recognition can be verified through the **Anabin database** (for academic degrees) or through the **Recognition Portal (Anerkennung in Deutschland)** for vocational qualifications
 - If the qualification is only partially equivalent, the applicant may be eligible for **adaptation or compensation training** to obtain full recognition
 Employment and Salary Requirements - The employment offer must be **directly related to the applicant’s qualification**
 Financial and Health Requirements - The applicant must be financially self-sufficient and able to support themselves and any dependents without relying on public assistance
 Obtain formal **recognition of qualification** (if required)
 Gather all supporting documents (proof of qualifications, recognition, salary, insurance, accommodation)
 - Recognized qualification or degree certificate
 - Proof of qualification recognition (Anabin/ZAB certificate)
 Ineligibility Conditions Applicants may be found ineligible if they: - Fail to prove qualification recognition or equivalence
 - Do not have a valid job offer aligned with their qualification
 - **Recognition Act (Anerkennungsgesetz)** – Recognition of foreign qualifications
 - Time spent working as a skilled professional counts toward permanent settlement eligibility
