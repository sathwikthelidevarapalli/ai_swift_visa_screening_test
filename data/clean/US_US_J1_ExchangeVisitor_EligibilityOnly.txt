 Basic Eligibility Requirements - The applicant must be **accepted into a U
 Department of State** to issue **Form DS-2019 (Certificate of Eligibility for Exchange Visitor Status)**
 - The applicant must **meet all eligibility and qualification requirements** set by the program sponsor
 - The applicant must have **adequate health insurance** that meets the requirements established by the U
 - The applicant must **maintain valid J-1 status** by complying with program terms and reporting requirements
 - The applicant must **depart the United States upon program completion** unless granted a change of status, extension, or waiver of the two-year home residency requirement (if applicable)
 Categories of Exchange Visitors The J-1 visa includes multiple program categories, each with distinct eligibility criteria and objectives: - **Students:** Enrolled in full-time degree or non-degree programs at accredited U
 - Most J-1 visa holders are subject to a **two-year home-country physical presence requirement** under INA §212(e), requiring them to return to their home country for at least two years after program completion before applying for certain U
 Key Documents Required - Form DS-2019 (Certificate of Eligibility for Exchange Visitor Status)
 Common Reasons for Ineligibility or Denial - Failure to be accepted into a State Department–designated exchange program
 - Failure to meet health insurance or sponsor-specific requirements
 - Inability to demonstrate intent to return to home country (nonimmigrant intent requirement)
