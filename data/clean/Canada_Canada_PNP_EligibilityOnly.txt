 Basic Eligibility Requirements - The applicant must possess **skills, education, and work experience** that will positively contribute to the **economy of the nominating province or territory**
 - Each province and territory has its own **specific eligibility criteria**, such as occupation lists, work experience thresholds, or language proficiency requirements
 - Processing times are longer than Express Entry, and applicants must meet **provincial eligibility criteria** but do not need to meet federal economic class program standards
 - Must meet the eligibility of at least one of the federal economic programs: • **Federal Skilled Worker Class (FSW)** • **Federal Skilled Trades Class (FST)** • **Canadian Experience Class (CEC)** - After provincial nomination, the applicant receives **600 additional CRS points**, substantially increasing chances of receiving an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residence
 Admissibility Requirements - Applicants and their accompanying family members must be **admissible to Canada** under the **Immigration and Refugee Protection Act (IRPA)** and **Regulations (IRPR)**
 Documentation and Application Requirements - **Nomination certificate** from the province or territory
 Common Reasons for Ineligibility - Failure to meet provincial nomination criteria
 - Nominees and their families may apply for **Canadian citizenship** after meeting permanent residence requirements and residency obligations
